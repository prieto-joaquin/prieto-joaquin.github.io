Hello. I am a Visiting Fellow at the <strong>International Inequalities Institute (<a href="https://www.lse.ac.uk/international-inequalities" target="_blank" rel="noopener">III</a>) at the LSE</strong> and currently work as a Postdoctoral Researcher at the <strong><a href="https://gobierno.uchile.cl/" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Faculty of Government</a> at the University of Chile</strong>, where I also coordinate the <strong><a href="https://diplomasuchile.cl/pobreza-y-desigualdad-herramientas-analiticas-para-su-estudio-y-la-formulacion-de-politicas-publicas-basadas-en-evidencia" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Diploma</a> in Poverty and Inequality</strong>. In addition, I work as a consultant for **ECLAC**, focusing on multidimensional inequality within the <a href="https://www.cepal.org/es/areas-trabajo/desarrollo-social" target="_blank" rel="noopener">Social Development Division</a>.

My main interests are the **evolution of welfare states**, labour instability, material and subjective well-being, and the institutional capacities that sustain effective systems of social protection. Within this framework, my research focuses on the measurement of poverty, inequality, and **new approaches to well-being**, as well as on economic insecurity in transforming labour markets and regional disparities shaped by territorial and institutional contexts.

I obtained a **PhD in Social Policy from the LSE**. I also hold a BA in Industrial Engineering, an MSc in Economy and Environmental Management, and postgraduate studies in Sociology from Pontificia Universidad Católica de Chile. Before joining the International Inequalities Institute, I founded and directed the Social Observatory at Universidad Alberto Hurtado in Chile, specializing in longitudinal data for social policy design, implementation, and monitoring.

I am a member of the <strong><a href="http://waplac.org/country-members" target="_blank" rel="noopener">WAPLAC Network</a></strong> (Welfare and Policy in Latin America and the Caribbean), <strong><a href="https://sites.google.com/view/weipo-uah/team" target="_blank" rel="noopener">WEIPO</a></strong> (Wellbeing, Inequality, Poverty and Public Policy group at the University of Alcalá de Henares, Spain), and the Work and Employment Thematic Group of the <strong><a href="https://hd-ca.org/thematic_group/work-employment" target="_blank" rel="noopener">HDCA</a></strong> (Human Development &amp; Capability Association). This year, I also co-organized the mini-conference <em>Social and Political Dynamics of Insecurity</em> at the <strong><a href="https://sase.org/events/2026-bordeaux/#mini-conferences" target="_blank" rel="noopener">SASE</a></strong> 2026 in Bordeaux.

My current work develops new well-being measures aimed at **improving public policy design** and contributing to more inclusive and sustainable development in emerging countries.
